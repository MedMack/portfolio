WUPWOO Project

Wupwoo is an exciting social game for Web and mobile where meeting people and finding out what makes them who they are can win you great prizes. Using your smart phone, you can play Wupwoo in teams, with your mates, or on your own. Win prizes, just for socialising.

Some information below are my own and does not necessarily reflect the views of Wupwoo Ltd.

My Role

I was hired as a (remote - freelance) UX/UI designer and front-end developer as a part of a small team to help design the v 2.0 of the project in October 2013.

Up until Jan 2015, I led efforts to evolve the user experience and the platform visual identity making the game easier to understand and enjoy.

Customer Insights & Ideation

I partnered with the project managers and a group of testers to uncover insights and translate concepts into features that address user behaviours and motivations.

Experience Strategy & Vision

I created wireframes and prototypes to share the vision, design principles and content strategy. This helped to evangelise ideas, gain alignment and drive decision making.

Oversight & Coordination

I designed across and collaborated with the project manager and the lead developer to translate ideas into new features.

Design Execution & Validation

I designed the project to have the same feel on navigators and mobile. I executed journeys, wireframes, prototypes and design specs.

The Challenge

The new release with one mission : bring a better experience to users

The first thing I noticed reviewing the old version of the project was confusion. I concept of the game took me a while to understand and I was wondering "how many other people felt the frustration while discovering the game ?"

Our challenge was to a total rework using new technologies and a new design for the website and the mobile app making the experience of the game easier and more performant. All along with new business partners to make Wupwoo a successful social game in the U.K.

The Approach

UI Re-Design

As we had less than a year, we were tasked to design and build some of the Wupwoo project within the existing architecture. This choice was perceived to be advantageous and the least riskiest for deployment later.

When it comes me, the idea was to focus on the game features UI and interactions. We spent the first two months breaking down elements, studying new ideas, prototyping and testing.

At this early stage, we were able to finish the case study of all the game features, succesfully implementing some of them gave us a taste of how the new version will be like.

Breaking down the project

The UI design and development of each feature were studied and re-designer seperatly. I led the design for all features including the in-game and the website related elements.

Each feature phase of the project was serialised, starting with the design then the development. Once each feature was designed and approved, the developer and I (with some of my web dev skills) began the implementation.

what's new in the v 2.0

bringing Game chat to mobile

The new verson came with a mobile chat as a new feature. The chat was a main feature in the web version, as we launched the mobile app, we decided to focus more on that.

The UI breakdown

The idea was to go for a clear and simple design. The tricky part in the chat design was the "ignore list". We went for a side bar where the user can drag and drop other users' icon to mute them in the chat.
